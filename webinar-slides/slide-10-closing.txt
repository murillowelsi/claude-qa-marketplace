  WHAT HASN'T CHANGED
  --------------------
  - Critical thinking about what can go wrong
  - Judgment about risk and coverage tradeoffs
  - Domain knowledge and understanding of user behavior
  - Accountability for release quality
  - Advocacy for the user experience

  WHAT HAS CHANGED
  ----------------
  - Repetitive documentation is handled by AI agents
  - Test plans are generated, not written from scratch
  - Bug reports are structured and complete by default
  - Risk prioritization is transparent and ISTQB-aligned
  - Story quality is caught before development, not after

  THE NEW QA SUPERPOWER
  ---------------------
  The best QA engineers in 2026 are not the ones who type fastest.
  They are the ones who know which questions to ask,
  which risks to escalate,
  and how to direct an AI agent to do the repetitive work
  while they focus on what matters.

  THE OUTCOME
  -----------
  Teams using AI-assisted QA workflows report:
    - 50%+ reduction in manual test creation time
    - Fewer defects reaching production
    - Faster sprint cycles with better story quality
    - QA engineers who are more engaged, less burned out
