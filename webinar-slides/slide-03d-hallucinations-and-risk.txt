================================================================================
  SLIDE 3D: HALLUCINATIONS, REASONING ERRORS & BIAS — THE HONEST SLIDE

================================================================================

  Before we demo the skills, we need to say the thing out loud.

  AI gets things wrong. Here is the ISTQB taxonomy of how.

  THE THREE FAILURE MODES
  -----------------------

  HALLUCINATION
    The model generates output that is plausible but factually incorrect.
    It does not know it is wrong. It does not flag uncertainty.
    "Plausible is not necessarily correct."

  REASONING ERROR
    The model follows a logical chain but reaches the wrong conclusion.
    ISTQB specifically warns: reasoning errors are most likely in
    test planning and test prioritization — exactly our domain.

  BIAS
    The model's training data shapes its outputs in ways that may not
    reflect your application, your users, or your risk profile.
    Coverage gaps can appear systematic rather than random.

  WHY THIS MATTERS FOR QA SPECIFICALLY
  --------------------------------------
  A hallucinated test case looks like a real test case.
  A biased risk assessment looks like a reasoned one.
  A plausible acceptance criteria rewrite can introduce ambiguity.

  If you hand AI output directly to a developer without review,
  these failure modes reach production undetected.

  HOW THE QA TOOLKIT MITIGATES EACH ONE
  ---------------------------------------

  PROMPT CHAINING
    Complex skills (like /analyze-story) break work into steps.
    Each step is verified before the next begins.
    Errors compound less when caught early.

  STRUCTURED OUTPUT FORMAT
    Forcing the model to produce a table, a severity rating, or a
    Given/When/Then forces it to commit to concrete, reviewable claims.
    Vague hallucinations struggle to survive structured templates.

  HUMAN REVIEW CHECKPOINT
    Every skill ends with a human reading the output.
    The tester is the last line of defense — and the most important one.
    The agent handles volume. The tester handles judgment.

  REAL INPUT DATA
    Skills fetch live Jira tickets, real browser sessions, actual
    test results. Working from grounded data reduces hallucination
    compared to prompts with no source material.

  THE RIGHT MENTAL MODEL
  ----------------------
  AI-generated testware is a first draft, not a final artifact.
  A first draft written in seconds is still a first draft.
  Your job as a QA engineer shifts from writing to reviewing —
  but reviewing remains non-negotiable.
